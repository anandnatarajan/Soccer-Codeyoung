History of Soccer

More than 240 million people around the world play soccer regularly according to the Federation Internationale de Football Association (FIFA). The game has evolved from the sport of kicking a rudimentary animal-hide ball around into the World Cup sport it is today. Records trace the history of soccer back more than 2,000 years ago to ancient China. Greece, Rome, and parts of Central America also claim to have started the sport; but it was England that transitioned soccer, or what the British and many other people around the world call “football,” into the game we know today. The English are credited with recording the first uniform rules for the sport, including forbidding tripping opponents and touching the ball with hands.



Basic Rules + How to Play

  1. No Hands

    You cannot use your hands in soccer. You are only allowed to use your feet and sometimes, head if you are not a goalie

  2. Throw-ins

    When a ball is kicked of the sidelines, the player on the team that did not kick it off the sidelines picks it up and throws it to teamates

  3. Corner & Goal Kicks

    When a ball is kicked or defended and rolls of the goal lines, then either a corner or goal kick happens. The goalie on the side it rolled off kicks it to a teamate(s). A corner kick is when a ball rolls into the goal line, but is kicked by the side defended.

  4. Fouls

    If a player is injured, then the referee will call a yellow card or a red card, and the injured player will be replaced by a substitute.

  5. Penalty Kicks

    Penalty Kicks will be given to a team if one of their players are injured by the other team. If the opposing team scores, then a goal is added to the team with an injured player.
